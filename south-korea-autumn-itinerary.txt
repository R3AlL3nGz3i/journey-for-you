====================================================================
    SOUTH KOREA AUTUMN FOLIAGE ITINERARY -- 5 DAYS IN OCTOBER
           A Romantic Journey for Two / 雙人秋旅韓國
====================================================================

----------------------------------------------------------------------
DAY 1 | 第一日：楓紅初遇，古宮秋韻
"First Encounter with Crimson Maples, Autumn Rhymes of Ancient Palaces"
Location Focus: Jongno-gu, Central Seoul
----------------------------------------------------------------------

MORNING | Gyeongbokgung Palace (景福宮)
  Arrive at Gyeongbokgung by 9:00 AM to witness the Royal Guard
  Changing Ceremony (매시간 교대식) at 10:00 AM. The palace grounds
  are framed by fiery maples and golden ginkgos in October. Rent a
  hanbok (Korean traditional dress) from Hanboknam (한복남) on the
  street outside Exit 5 -- wearing hanbok grants you free palace
  entry and makes for extraordinary couple photos against the
  Gyeonghoeru Pavilion reflected in its lotus pond.

AFTERNOON | Bukchon Hanok Village & Ikseon-dong (北村韓屋村 / 益善洞)
  Walk north from Gyeongbokgung into Bukchon Hanok Village. Climb
  to Bukchon Viewpoint #5 and #6 for the iconic rooftop panorama.
  Then stroll south to Ikseon-dong, Seoul's oldest hanok neighborhood
  reimagined as a labyrinth of tiny cafes, vintage shops, and art
  galleries hidden in 1920s courtyard houses.

EVENING | Cheonggyecheon Stream Night Walk (清溪川)
  After dinner, walk along the illuminated Cheonggyecheon Stream,
  an 11-km restored urban waterway. In October, the stream often
  hosts the Seoul Lantern Festival (서울빛초롱축제), with thousands
  of handmade lanterns glowing along the water.

RESTAURANT RECOMMENDATION:
  Seoul Gyeongyangsik 1920 (서울경양식1920) -- Ikseon-dong
  A retro-chic hanok restaurant serving "gyeongyangsik" (Korean-style
  Western cuisine from the 1920s). Order the hambak steak (함박스테이크)
  and the cream croquettes. The candlelit hanok courtyard is deeply
  romantic. Located on Ikseon-dong's main alley.

SECRET MOMENT:
  At Gyeongbokgung, skip the main halls. Instead, walk to the far
  northwest corner to find Hyangwonjeong Pavilion (香遠亭) -- a
  hexagonal pavilion on a small island connected by a wooden bridge,
  surrounded by autumn foliage with almost no crowds. This is the
  single most photogenic spot in all of Seoul's palaces in autumn.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
DAY 2 | 第二日：秘苑深處，紅葉如詩
"Deep Within the Secret Garden, Red Leaves Like Poetry"
Location Focus: Changdeokgung & Namsan, Seoul
----------------------------------------------------------------------

MORNING | Changdeokgung Secret Garden (昌德宮後苑)
  Book the Secret Garden guided tour (Huwon) in advance -- tickets
  open at 10:00 AM KST, 6 days before your visit date, via the
  Cultural Heritage Administration website (reservation.cha.go.kr).
  The 78-acre garden is the crown jewel of Korean autumn: 300-year-old
  trees reflected in Buyongji Pond, scholar pavilions draped in maple
  canopy, and stone pathways carpeted in fallen leaves. English tours
  run at 10:30 AM and 2:30 PM. Limited to 50 people per tour.

AFTERNOON | Namsan Tower Sunset (南山塔)
  Take the Namsan Cable Car from the base station (near Myeongdong)
  up to N Seoul Tower. Arrive by 4:30 PM to catch golden hour. Buy
  a love lock from the vendors at the base and lock it to the
  terrace railing together -- a Seoul couples' tradition. The
  observation deck offers 360-degree views as the city transitions
  from golden afternoon light to glittering nightscape.

EVENING | Myeongdong Street Food & Shopping (明洞)
  Descend from Namsan into Myeongdong for the legendary night street
  food market. Must-try items: egg bread (gyeran-bbang), tornado
  potato, hotteok (sweet pancakes), and tteokbokki. Browse Korean
  skincare shops -- Innisfree, Olive Young, and Sulwhasoo flagship.

RESTAURANT RECOMMENDATION:
  Myeongdong Kyoja (명동교자) -- Myeongdong
  A Seoul institution since 1966. Order the kalguksu (hand-cut knife
  noodles in rich broth) and the mandu (dumplings). Cash only. There
  will be a line -- it moves fast and is absolutely worth it. Located
  on Myeongdong 10-gil.

SECRET MOMENT:
  At Namsan Tower, instead of the main observation deck, find the
  outdoor "Roof Terrace" on the top level. At exactly sunset, the
  tower's LED lights begin their evening display, and you can watch
  the sun set behind Bukhansan Mountain while standing above the
  entire glowing city. Bring a small bottle of Korean sparkling wine
  (sold at the tower's convenience store) for a private toast.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
DAY 3 | 第三日：島嶼金黃，銀杏隧道
"Island of Gold, Tunnel of Ginkgo"
Location Focus: Nami Island & Gapyeong (加平)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

MORNING | Nami Island Ginkgo Tree Tunnel (南怡島)
  Depart Seoul early (7:30 AM) from Yongsan Station. Take the ITX
  Cheongchun train to Gapyeong Station (약 1hr 15min). From
  Gapyeong, take the shuttle bus or taxi to the Nami Island wharf,
  then the ferry (or zipline!) across. Walk the famous Metasequoia
  Lane first, then find the Riverside Lover's Ginkgo Tree Lane on
  the island's south side -- in late October, these trees form a
  cathedral of pure gold above a carpet of fallen leaves. This is
  the filming location of "Winter Sonata" and one of Korea's most
  romantic landscapes.

AFTERNOON | The Garden of Morning Calm (아침고요수목원)
  Take the Gapyeong shuttle bus to The Garden of Morning Calm, a
  sprawling botanical garden about 30 minutes from Nami Island.
  In autumn, the garden's 5,000+ plant species create layered
  walls of orange, red, and gold. The Sunken Garden and Moonlight
  Garden sections are particularly stunning. In the evening hours,
  the garden sometimes hosts autumn illumination festivals.

EVENING | Return to Seoul & Chimaek Night
  Return to Seoul via ITX train. Head to a chimaek (chicken + beer)
  spot to experience one of Korea's most beloved social rituals.

RESTAURANT RECOMMENDATION:
  BBQ Chicken Hongdae (비비큐 치킨 홍대) or, for a more local
  experience, Kyochon Chicken Hongdae Branch (교촌치킨 홍대점).
  Order the honey garlic chicken (허니갈릭) and pair with draft
  Cass or Terra beer. Located in Hongdae's main strip area near
  Hongik University Station Exit 9.

SECRET MOMENT:
  At Nami Island, rent a tandem bicycle and ride to the island's
  far eastern tip where a small, unmarked beach faces the river.
  In October mornings, mist rises from the water through the golden
  trees. Almost no one walks this far. Sit on the rocks together
  and watch the fog dissolve into sunlight.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
DAY 4 | 第四日：雪嶽丹楓，山巒似錦
"Crimson Maples of Seoraksan, Mountains Like Brocade"
Location Focus: Seoraksan National Park (雪嶽山國立公園)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

MORNING | Seoraksan National Park Hiking
  Option A (Guided Day Tour): Book a day tour departing Seoul at
  6:00-7:00 AM (available via GetYourGuide or Klook, ~3.5 hours
  each way by bus).
  Option B (Independent): Take the KTX from Seoul Station to
  Sokcho (속초) -- about 2.5 hours, then taxi to the park entrance.

  Start with the Biseondae Trail (飛仙臺): a gentle 3.5 km riverside
  walk from the park entrance through Sinheungsa Temple (신흥사)
  to Biseondae Rock. The trail follows a crystal stream through a
  valley of blazing maples. Stop at Sinheungsa to see the massive
  bronze seated Buddha backed by autumn mountains.

AFTERNOON | Seoraksan Cable Car & Gwongeumseong Fortress
  Take the Seoraksan Cable Car (설악 케이블카) to Gwongeumseong
  Fortress ruins at 700m elevation. Book tickets online in advance
  at the Seorak Cable Car website -- they sell out in autumn. From
  the top, the panorama of Seoraksan's peaks dressed in red, orange,
  and gold is breathtaking. On clear days, you can see the East Sea.

EVENING | Sokcho Seafood Dinner
  Before returning to Seoul, eat at Sokcho's famous seafood district
  near Sokcho Tourist & Fishery Market (속초관광수산시장). Try fresh
  raw fish (hoe/회), grilled shellfish, and sundae (Korean blood
  sausage).

RESTAURANT RECOMMENDATION:
  Bongpo Meokgukjip (봉포머국집) -- Sokcho waterfront area
  A beloved local restaurant famous for moguk (모국), a clear
  radish and beef broth soup, and fresh sashimi platters. Order
  the modeum hoe (모둠회, assorted sashimi) and a steaming bowl
  of moguk to warm up after hiking. The restaurant overlooks the
  harbor.

SECRET MOMENT:
  At Sinheungsa Temple on the Biseondae trail, look for the small
  side path marked "Gyejoam Hermitage" (계조암). It leads to a tiny
  cliffside hermitage built into the rock face with a natural cave
  shrine. Few hikers take this detour. Ring the small bell together
  and make a wish -- temple tradition says wishes made by couples
  here are granted.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
DAY 5 | 第五日：湯泉暖意，最後秋吻
"Warm Springs and Tender Warmth, A Final Autumn Kiss"
Location Focus: Hongdae, Hapjeong & Seoul Farewell
----------------------------------------------------------------------

MORNING | Jjimjilbang Spa Experience
  Start the final day with a jjimjilbang visit. Recommended:

  - Siloam Sauna (실로암사우나) near Seoul Station -- a massive
    multi-floor spa with jade rooms, salt rooms, ice rooms, and
    a rooftop rest area. Very clean, foreigner-friendly, open 24hrs.
    Note: bathing areas are gender-separated, but the jjimjilbang
    (clothed sauna/lounge) floors are co-ed, so couples can relax
    together in matching uniforms, eat baked eggs (맥반석 계란),
    drink sikhye (sweet rice drink), and nap on heated floors.

  Alternative: Spa Land at Starfield COEX if you prefer a more
  upscale, modern experience.

AFTERNOON | Hongdae & Hapjeong Neighborhood Stroll
  Explore Hongdae's indie art scene -- buskers perform in Hongdae
  Playground, murals cover every alley, and vintage shops line
  Eoulmadang-ro. Walk to neighboring Hapjeong for a quieter, more
  curated cafe culture. Visit Anthracite Coffee Roasters in a
  converted shoe factory -- one of Seoul's most beautiful cafes.

EVENING | Korean BBQ Farewell Dinner & Hongdae Nightlife
  End the trip with the quintessential Korean experience: grilling
  meat together at a Korean BBQ restaurant, wrapping it in lettuce
  with garlic, ssamjang, and a sliver of raw chili.

RESTAURANT RECOMMENDATION:
  Musoe Kimchi Samgyeop (무쇠김치삼겹) -- Hongdae, Mapo-gu
  Located at 1 Donggyo-ro 30-gil. They grill thick-cut samgyeopsal
  (pork belly) on cast-iron pans with aged kimchi that caramelizes
  alongside the meat. Order the kimchi samgyeopsal set and add a
  doenjang-jjigae (fermented soybean stew) to share. Pair with soju
  or makgeolli. Casual, smoky, and deeply satisfying. This is the
  real Korea.

SECRET MOMENT:
  After dinner, walk 10 minutes south from Hongdae to the Gyeongui
  Line Forest Park (경의선숲길), a converted railway track turned
  into a narrow linear park. In October, the trees along the old
  tracks glow amber under string lights. Find a bench, share a can
  of warm coffee from a vending machine, and watch couples and
  buskers drift past. It is an unexpectedly peaceful farewell to
  a city that never stops moving.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
BEST AREAS TO STAY IN SEOUL
----------------------------------------------------------------------

1. MYEONGDONG (明洞) -- Best for first-timers & convenience
   Central location, walking distance to Namsan and palaces.
   Hotels: L'Escape Hotel, L7 Myeongdong by Lotte, Nine Tree Hotel
   Price range: 80,000-200,000 KRW/night ($60-$150 USD)

2. JONGNO-GU (鍾路區) -- Best for culture & palace proximity
   Steps from Gyeongbokgung, Changdeokgung, Bukchon, Ikseon-dong.
   Hotels: Nines Hotel Myeongdong, Shilla Stay Gwanghwamun
   Boutique hanok stays available via Airbnb in Bukchon.
   Price range: 70,000-180,000 KRW/night ($50-$135 USD)

3. HONGDAE (弘大) -- Best for nightlife, food & young energy
   Vibrant, walkable, excellent BBQ and bar scene.
   Hotels: Ryse Autograph Collection, L7 Hongdae by Lotte
   Price range: 60,000-150,000 KRW/night ($45-$110 USD)

4. HANNAM-DONG / ITAEWON -- Best for upscale/trendy couples
   Boutique cafes, galleries, international dining.
   Price range: 100,000-300,000 KRW/night ($75-$220 USD)

----------------------------------------------------------------------
PACKING ESSENTIALS FOR OCTOBER KOREA
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  CLOTHING:
  - Light jacket or windbreaker (daily essential)
  - 1 warm fleece or sweater (for Seoraksan and evenings)
  - Light scarf (doubles as style accessory and warmth)
  - Comfortable walking shoes / light hiking shoes
  - 1 pair of jeans, 1-2 pairs of casual trousers
  - Layers: long-sleeve shirts, cardigans (10-22C range)
  - 1 dressier outfit for evening dining

  ESSENTIALS:
  - Sunglasses and sunscreen (autumn sun is deceptively strong)
  - Lip balm (dry mountain air)
  - Compact umbrella (3-5 rain days typical in October)
  - Portable charger / power bank
  - Universal adapter (Korea uses Type C and F, 220V)
  - Reusable water bottle
  - Small daypack for hiking

  NICE TO HAVE:
  - Sheet masks (or buy in Myeongdong for pennies)
  - Warm socks for jjimjilbang
  - Camera with extra memory cards (you will take thousands
    of autumn photos)

  TEMPERATURES:
  - Early October: 18-22C day / 10-14C night
  - Late October: 12-16C day / 6-10C night
  - Mountains (Seoraksan): 5-8C cooler than Seoul

TRANSPORT TIPS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  T-MONEY CARD (교통카드):
  - Buy at any convenience store (CU, GS25, 7-Eleven) for 2,500 KRW
  - Top up at convenience stores or subway station machines
  - Works on all subways, buses, and even some taxis
  - Slightly cheaper per ride than buying single tickets
  - Alternatively, get the "Climate Card" tourist pass: unlimited
    daily transport from ~4,500 KRW/day

  KTX (HIGH-SPEED RAIL):
  - Book via the Korail website (letskorail.com) or the Korail app
  - Seoul to Sokcho (for Seoraksan): ~2.5 hours via KTX/ITX
  - Seoul to Gapyeong (for Nami Island): ~1hr 15min via ITX Cheongchun
    from Yongsan or Cheongnyangni Station
  - Tip: Book KTX tickets 1 month in advance for October travel;
    autumn foliage season sells out fast

  AIRPORT TO CITY:
  - Incheon Airport to Seoul: AREX (Airport Railroad Express)
    Direct train to Seoul Station: 45 min, ~9,500 KRW
    All-stop train: 58 min, ~4,750 KRW

  GENERAL TIPS:
  - Seoul's subway system is world-class, clean, and English-friendly
  - Naver Map or KakaoMap are far more accurate than Google Maps
    in Korea -- download before arrival
  - Kakao T app for taxis (Korea's Uber equivalent)
  - Most places accept card payment; cash needed for some street
    food vendors and traditional markets

BEST FOLIAGE TIMING FORECAST
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  The Korea Meteorological Administration (KMA) releases the official
  autumn foliage forecast in early September each year. Based on
  historical patterns and recent trends:

  REGION               FIRST COLOR      PEAK FOLIAGE
  -------------------------------------------------------
  Seoraksan (설악산)    Late Sep         Oct 15-25
  Odaesan (오대산)      Early Oct        Oct 20-28
  Seoul City           Mid Oct          Oct 28 - Nov 7
  Nami Island/Gapyeong Mid Oct          Oct 25 - Nov 5
  Naejangsan (내장산)   Mid Oct          Nov 1-10
  Gyeongju             Late Oct         Nov 5-15
  -------------------------------------------------------

  RECOMMENDED TRAVEL WINDOW: October 18-27

  This window captures Seoraksan at or near peak, Nami Island
  approaching peak gold, and Seoul palaces beginning their most
  vibrant transformation. For the absolute best overlap across
  all destinations in this itinerary, aim for the fourth week
  of October (Oct 20-27).

  Check the KMA forecast (weather.go.kr) starting in early
  September for the exact 2026 dates, as temperatures in
  August-September influence timing by 3-5 days.
